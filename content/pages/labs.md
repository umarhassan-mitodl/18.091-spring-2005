These labs are based on those given in Professor Robert L. Devaney's textbook, *A First Course in Chaotic Dynamical Systems*. New York, NY: Addison-Wesley Publishing Company, The Advanced Book Program, 1995.Â ISBN: 0201554062, and in his course at Boston University. They utilize the resources provided by him on {{% resource_link "7c944095-66ae-4ea7-b299-fefbdb13edaa" "The Dynamical Systems and Technology Project Web site" %}} and {{% resource_link "ffee3200-832e-4a7e-aee6-82a17c35902d" "Mathematica Tools" %}} written by Sebastian Marotta.

Students are strongly encouraged to work together on these experiments. In each computer lab, students are asked to write an essay describing their findings. See the {{% resource_link "3df6ff7f-b24c-c1c5-fc8e-789825b79373" "related resources" %}} section for information on writing mathematics and Lab Report Expectations ({{% resource_link "e6f3ee9e-564f-b37e-c6d8-0198bdf74862" "PDF" %}}).
